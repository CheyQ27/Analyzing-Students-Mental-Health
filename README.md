# International Students Mental Health Analysis

## 📘 Overview
This project explores whether studying in a different country affects students’ mental health.  
Using the `students` dataset, we analyze factors such as social connectedness, acculturative stress, and length of stay to see how they relate to depression scores.

## 🧠 Objectives
- Explore mental‑health patterns among international students  
- Examine whether social connectedness and acculturative stress predict depression  
- Investigate whether length of stay influences mental‑health outcomes  

## 🗄️ Dataset
**File:** students.csv  
**Description:** Contains student demographic information and mental‑health‑related scores.  

## 🛠️ Tools
- PostgreSQL  
- SQL queries for data cleaning and analysis  

## 📝 Key Queries
- Creating the students table  
- Summaries of depression, stress, and connectedness  
- Grouping by international status or length of stay  
- Correlation‑style comparisons using SQL aggregates  

## 📈 Insights
- Students with **longer lengths of stay** tended to show **lower PHQ depression scores**, suggesting better mental‑health outcomes over time.  
- Students with **shorter stays** showed **higher social connectedness (SCS)** and **higher acculturative stress (AS)** scores, indicating that early‑stage adjustment may involve both stronger social needs and greater cultural stress.
